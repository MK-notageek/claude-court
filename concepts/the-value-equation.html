01
THE VALUE EQUATIONTwo Moves

Everyone says “add value.” Nobody defines it.

“Give value. Provide more value.” Great — but what is value? What actually makes a product worth more, or less?

There are two moves. First you build something valuable. Then you enhance it — with scarcity, urgency, bonuses, guarantees, or simply a sharper name.

So we’ll do both: the equation that defines value, then the enhancer with the most leverage — guarantees.

02
THE VALUE EQUATIONThe Formula

Value, written as a fraction.

Four variables. Two create value, two destroy it. Draw a line down the middle of the page — two on top, two on the bottom.

Do both and you’ve built something people pay a premium for — with margins to match. Most founders only ever work the top.

03
THE VALUE EQUATIONThe Top Half

The top half — what creates value.

Dream outcome: the thing they actually want. It separates whole industries — “make a million” out-prices “lose weight,” which is why B2B out-prices B2C.

Perceived likelihood: do they believe it’ll work for them? Amazon’s reviews do exactly this — they raise belief, so people pay. This top half is the easy half, and where most founders stop.

04
THE VALUE EQUATIONThe Bottom Half

The bottom half — what destroys value.

Time delay: how long between paying and getting. Two agencies, same promise — one rings your phone in 60 days, the other before your card finishes processing.

Effort & sacrifice: what they must start doing and stop doing. Push both toward zero. And deliver a result first — not the biggest, just a tangible one they can hold.

05
THE VALUE EQUATIONThe Moat

The fortunes live on the bottom.

Bigger promises and more testimonials are easy — anyone can do it. Delivering faster, with less effort, takes real operational muscle. That’s the moat.

06
THE VALUE EQUATIONIt’s Also Your Copy

The same four variables are your copy.

Value and persuasion are married. The four variables don’t just build the offer — they write the headline.

Pull the outcome, a number for proof, the time it takes, and the effort it spares. Now you have headlines for ads, emails, sales pages, and hooks.

07
THE VALUE EQUATIONBuild It

Build it: list, solve, trim & stack.

Write down every micro-problem a customer hits on the way to the outcome. Then write the dream solution to each — no limits. You’ll end up with a hundred.

What survives the trim is high-value, high-margin — the offer you actually want to sell.

08
GUARANTEESThe Real Enemy

You’re not selling against rivals.

You’re selling against inaction. Doubt. The comfort of doing nothing. The real enemy is risk — so lower the bar until they can just step over it.

You’ve done this a hundred times; it’s their first. You know more about the outcome than they do — so it’s only fair the risk sits with the side that knows. That’s a guarantee.

09
GUARANTEESThe Four Types

The four guarantees.

A guarantee is purchase insurance — you assume part of the buyer’s risk. Here are the four ways to structure one.

10
GUARANTEESEngineer It

Then engineer it to win.

Stack them: a 30-day unconditional and a 90-day conditional. The sale becomes a trial — more closes, a few more refunds, net more money.

And spell it out. Vague guarantees get banner-blindness. Name the exact outcome — you don’t even need the word “guarantee.”
