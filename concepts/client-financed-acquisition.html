CLIENT-FINANCED ACQUISITIONThe Problem

Growth costs money. Until it doesn’t.

The standard playbook: spend $100 to acquire a customer, hope they’re worth $300 over time, and pray you survive the gap.

Most businesses die in that gap. They’re profitable on paper — their unit economics work — but they run out of cash before the lifetime value arrives. Scale accelerates the bleeding.

The fix isn’t a bigger war chest. It’s re-engineering the timing. Make the money arrive before — or the instant — you spend it.

02
CLIENT-FINANCED ACQUISITIONThe Insight

Customer #1 buys customer #2.

The constraint isn’t growth — it’s the sequence of cash. Flip the sequence and growth funds itself. The front-end of your offer stack covers your acquisition cost; the back-end is where you profit.

This isn’t a loan, a grant, or a new investor. Your customers become your growth capital. Every dollar they spend on entry goes straight back into getting the next customer through the door.

CLIENT-FINANCED ACQUISITIONThe SLO Math

The self-liquidating offer.

A Self-Liquidating Offer (SLO) is a low-cost product positioned right after your lead magnet. Its price matches your cost per lead. The ad spend disappears — liquidated — before the real sale even begins.

Classic numbers: $10 lead magnet opt-in, $37 SLO, $500 core offer. If your cost-per-lead is $30, the SLO covers it. The core offer is pure margin.

The SLO doesn’t need to be hugely profitable. It needs to break even. That’s the whole game — break even on acquisition, keep everything else.

04
CLIENT-FINANCED ACQUISITIONThe Three Layers

Stack three layers, each funding the next.

The full structure has three moves. The lead magnet collects attention for free or near-free. The SLO converts that attention into a small purchase that pays for the lead. The core offer is where you actually build wealth.

Each layer does a job. The lead magnet proves you can help. The SLO proves they will pay. The core offer proves how much. Don’t skip to the third — earn it with the first two.

CLIENT-FINANCED ACQUISITIONBefore vs After

Same month. Different math.

Without CFA, spending $10,000 on ads means $10,000 out the door before a single dollar returns. With CFA, the SLO revenue hits the same month — often the same week — as the ad spend.

The constraint changes completely. The old business has a capital problem. The CFA business has a conversion problem — a solvable one, with clear levers.

06
CLIENT-FINANCED ACQUISITIONUnit Economics

Run the numbers first.

CFA only works if the unit math holds. Three things must be true: your SLO conversion rate is high enough to cover your cost per click, your core offer converts a meaningful share of SLO buyers, and the back-end is priced to build real wealth.

Typical benchmarks: 20–40% of opt-ins buy the SLO. 5–15% of SLO buyers take the core offer. From there, small improvements in either number compound rapidly.

The lever people ignore: raise the SLO price. A $27 SLO that converts 30% of opt-ins at a $12 cost-per-click doesn’t liquidate. A $47 SLO at the same rate does.

07
CLIENT-FINANCED ACQUISITIONThe Flywheel

Once it liquidates, the flywheel turns.

Break-even acquisition is not the endgame — it’s the launch condition. Once the SLO covers your ad bill, you can reinvest all back-end profit into more ads. More ads bring more opt-ins. More opt-ins produce more SLO revenue. More SLO revenue funds more ads.

The ceiling is no longer how much cash you have. The ceiling is how large your market is. That’s a better problem to have.

08
CLIENT-FINANCED ACQUISITIONWhat Breaks It

Three things that kill the loop.

CFA fails in predictable ways. Each failure mode has one fix.

✗SLO too cheap: $7 SLO on a $25 CPL. Math never works. Raise the price or lower the cost.
✗SLO disconnected: lead magnet promises health tips, SLO sells productivity software. Buyers bounce. Match the promise exactly.
✗No back-end: the SLO becomes the core offer. Margins are thin. Customers have no place to go deeper. Build the next layer first.
✓Price to liquidate. Align all layers. Build the back-end before you scale the front. Then pour fuel on it.
