THE DIVERGENT LISTWhy Shallow Offers Lose

One solution. A dozen reasons to walk.

Every buyer has a dream outcome. Between them and it: a road full of obstacles. Solve only the headline problem and all the others become objections.

The shallow offer answers the loudest fear and stops. The buyer nods, then quietly thinks of nine more reasons this won’t work. Each unanswered obstacle is a reason to delay, hesitate, or leave.

The deep offer anticipates the full journey and removes friction at every stage. The buyer runs out of objections before they finish hearing it.

02
THE DIVERGENT LISTThe Three Steps

Three moves. One unrefusable offer.

The divergent list is a three-phase process. Two phases are this document. The third is its sequel.

Phase 1 — List: Write every obstacle between the customer and their dream outcome. No filter. The goal is exhaustion, not selection.

Phase 2 — Dream: For each obstacle, write the absolute best solution — what you’d do if cost and time were irrelevant. Still no filter.

Phase 3 — Trim & Stack: Score each solution on value vs. delivery cost. Keep only the stars. Bundle them into the offer. That’s the next breakdown.

03
THE DIVERGENT LISTPhase 1 — Map Every Obstacle

What could stop them at any point?

Take the dream outcome. Trace the entire path back to where the customer is today. That path is not a straight line — it is a sequence of decision points, skill gaps, logistics problems, and moments of doubt.

Write them all down: the obstacles before they start, the ones on day one, the ones at week three when motivation falls, and the hardest ones just before they succeed.

Do not stop until the list feels excessive. A hundred items is correct. Thirty is almost certainly too few — you stopped when it got uncomfortable.

04
THE DIVERGENT LISTPhase 2 — Dream Solutions

If cost didn’t matter, what would you do?

For each obstacle, write the most complete solution you can imagine — not the most practical. The one a thoughtful buyer would design if given unlimited resources.

This forces you away from cheap shortcuts and toward genuine value. And often the dream solution is cheaper to deliver than it first appears.

THE DIVERGENT LISTThe Four Types of Obstacles

All obstacles are one of four kinds.

Understanding which type an obstacle is tells you which delivery form solves it cheapest — and which ones buyers value most highly.

Internal blocks (mindset, belief, confidence) respond best to community, coaching, and early wins. External blocks (time, money, technology) respond to systems, tools, and done-for-you delivery.

Pre-existing problems walk in with the buyer. Process-created problems appear after they start. Solve both and you build trust at every stage of the journey.

06
THE DIVERGENT LISTDelivery Forms

The same solution. Many delivery forms.

Once you have the ideal solution for an obstacle, decide how it reaches the customer. Each delivery form has a different cost-to-perceived-value ratio.

Done-for-you has the highest perceived value but also the highest delivery cost. Digital assets have near-zero marginal cost. Most high-margin businesses live in the middle row.

A weekly group call costs you 90 minutes once and serves 40 people. A 10-page PDF costs nothing after creation. Match the delivery form to your economics — not to what feels most impressive.

07
THE DIVERGENT LISTThe Principle

Generate before you judge.

The failure mode is filtering too early. You build a short list, look at the first few solutions, conclude they cost too much, and stop. The result is a thin offer that competes on price.

A 10-item list is a product. A 100-item list trimmed to 10 is a Grand Slam Offer — because the items that survived the cut are genuinely the best available.

Think of it as a diamond: spread wide first, then narrow hard. The narrowing only works if the spreading was real. A shallow well never produces clean water.

08
THE DIVERGENT LISTDo It Now

Open a blank page. Start listing.

Write the dream outcome at the top. Walk the journey. List every obstacle, however small. Then write the best possible solution for each — no edits, no filters, no budgets.

✗Start by brainstorming solutions — you’ll build the first idea that comes to mind, not the best one.
✓Start with the full list of problems first — every obstacle to the dream outcome, exhaustively, before writing a single solution.
