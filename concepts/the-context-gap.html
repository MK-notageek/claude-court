VIBEERAThe Context Gap

It has happened to all of us.

Has it ever happened that you're trying to explain something to a teacher — and they just don't get it?

You knew exactly what you meant. It was crystal clear in your own head.

But they didn't have the context. And you couldn't phrase it right. So it came out as noise.

That gap — vague language plus missing context — is the single reason 99% of people never get what they want.

Not talent. Not effort. Not luck.

They know what they mean. The other person doesn't have the context to receive it. The signal dies in the gap.

02
VIBEERAThe Context Gap

Context is layered. You can't skip floors.

Let me ask you something simple. Can you teach calculus to someone who doesn't know basic exponents?

No. You can't.

Not because they're stupid — because the foundation isn't there. You'd be pouring water into a cup that hasn't been built yet.

Context works exactly the same way. It's built in layers — and you can't hand over the top one if the ones beneath it aren't there yet.

Here's how this hits us directly. When I tell you something, you're receiving it with maybe 5% of the context I'm operating from. Maybe less.

Not because you're not sharp. Because you haven't lived the last three years inside my head.

So when something I say lands as "obvious" to me and "vague" to you — that's not a you-problem or a me-problem. It's a gap problem.
