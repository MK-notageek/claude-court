THE FOUR LEAD GETTERSThe Single-Channel Trap

Most businesses run on one channel.

If that channel dries up — an algorithm shifts, a list goes cold, a platform changes its rules — the business stops growing. That’s not a strategy. It’s a dependency.

There are exactly four ways to acquire a lead. Not ten, not a hundred. Four. Every tactic you can name slots into one of them. Once you see the map, you stop treating channels as separate religions and start treating them as tools.

02
THE FOUR LEAD GETTERSThe Map

Two axes. Four quadrants.

The first axis is who you’re talking to: people you know (warm) or strangers (cold). The second is who does the work: you, or someone/something else.

Cross those two and you get exactly four strategies. Every ad campaign, every podcast, every DM, every referral fits here. None is better in the abstract — each has a moment where it’s the right tool.

03
THE FOUR LEAD GETTERSWarm Outreach

Channel 1: Warm outreach.

This is the fastest path to revenue that exists. You know these people. They already have a reason to take your call.

Go through your contacts — phone, social media, email — and sort them into three buckets: buyers, referrers, and reconnects. Then message each with a relevant reason to talk, not a pitch.

Most people skip this because it feels uncomfortable to sell to people they know. That discomfort is where the money is.

04
THE FOUR LEAD GETTERSPost Content

Channel 2: Post content.

You create it once. The platform distributes it forever. This is the only channel that builds a compounding asset — each post adds to a library that keeps generating leads long after you publish it.

The cost is time and consistency, not money. The payoff is inbound leads who already trust you before they ever send a message.

Content does not replace outreach. It amplifies it. When someone gets your DM and then sees your content, the trust compounds. That’s the lever.

05
THE FOUR LEAD GETTERSCold Outreach

Channel 3: Cold outreach.

You contact strangers who match your ideal customer profile. No relationship, no referral — just a targeted message to a qualified list.

It’s a volume game with a quality filter. 1,000 emails might yield 30 replies and 5 sales calls. That’s not failure — that’s the model. The math works when the offer is strong and the list is right.

The single biggest mistake: selling in the first message. Cold outreach opens a conversation. It does not close a deal.

06
THE FOUR LEAD GETTERSPaid Ads

Channel 4: Paid ads.

Paid ads let you trade money for speed. You reach strangers at scale, immediately, without waiting for content to compound or lists to warm up.

The catch: they expose your offer to reality instantly. A weak offer fails fast and expensively. A strong offer scales fast and profitably. Paid ads are a pressure test before they’re a growth engine.

Start with the offer. Then test cheap. Then scale what wins.

07
THE FOUR LEAD GETTERSThe Sequence

Start here. Build in order.

The four channels are not equal at every stage. Warm outreach first — it’s free, fast, and proves your offer. Then add cold outreach for volume. Then content for compounding. Then ads to pour fuel on a proven fire.

Most founders do this backwards — they buy ads before they know their offer converts, then blame the channel.

✗Run paid ads on a new, unproven offer. Spend $10k learning the hard way.
✓Prove the offer with warm outreach for free. Then pour paid ads on a model that already works.

THE FOUR LEAD GETTERSYour Lead System

Four channels. One system.

Warm outreach gives you speed. Cold outreach gives you scale. Content gives you compounding authority. Paid ads give you leverage. Running all four means no single channel failure can stop your growth.

Start with one. Master it until it produces leads consistently. Then add the next. Never spread so thin you do all four badly.
