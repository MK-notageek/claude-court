01
LTGP : CACThe Ratio

One ratio. Every business.

Lifetime Gross Profit divided by Customer Acquisition Cost. That fraction tells you how many dollars you make for every dollar you spend getting a customer.

A ratio of 1:1 means you break even on acquisition — growth costs you nothing, but earns you nothing either. Below 1:1 and you’re buying customers at a loss. Above 3:1 and you have a machine worth feeding.

The ratio doesn’t lie. Every pricing decision, every retention initiative, every ad dollar and referral program is ultimately in service of widening this gap.

02
LTGP : CACLifetime Gross Profit

LTGP: every dollar a customer ever gives you.

Lifetime Gross Profit is not revenue. It’s what’s left after the direct cost of delivering your product or service across the entire relationship. If a client pays $1,000/month and stays 18 months, and delivery costs $300/month, their LTGP is $12,600.

Three variables move LTGP: price, margin, and duration. Any one of them, raised, expands the top of the ratio without touching what you spend on acquisition.

Most founders try to fix their unit economics through more sales. The quieter path is raising LTGP on the customers they already have.

03
LTGP : CACCustomer Acquisition Cost

CAC: the real cost of one new customer.

Customer Acquisition Cost is all the money spent to get one paying client: ads, sales salaries, commissions, tools, agency fees, even the founder’s time. Divide total spend by customers acquired. That’s your CAC.

Most founders undercount it. They track ad spend but not the SDR salary, the CRM cost, or the two hours they spent on calls that didn’t close. An understated CAC is a lie you tell yourself.

The lower your CAC, the more of each LTGP dollar you keep as margin to fund growth, hire, or distribute as profit.

04
LTGP : CACThe 3:1 Rule

The threshold: 3:1 or bust.

Hormozi’s floor is LTGP:CAC ≥ 3:1. For every dollar spent on acquisition, earn at least three dollars of lifetime gross profit. Below 3:1 you’re grinding to stay in place — growth drains cash faster than profit refills it.

At 3:1 you have margin to reinvest, hire, and weather variance. At 5:1 you can be aggressive — pour fuel on growth and it lights fast. Above 10:1 either your prices are too low or you’re not investing enough in acquisition.

05
LTGP : CACRaising the Top

Four ways to raise LTGP without more customers.

The fastest path to a healthier ratio isn’t always cheaper ads. It’s making the customer you already have worth more over their lifetime.

Raise the price. Extend the duration through better onboarding and proactive results. Add upsells so customers naturally buy more as they succeed. And cut delivery cost without cutting quality. Any combination works — pick the lever with the least friction for your model.

06
LTGP : CACShrinking the Bottom

Lower CAC: make getting customers cheaper.

The other side of the ratio is the denominator. Three paths bring CAC down without starving the top of funnel.

Referrals: a customer who sends a friend has near-zero acquisition cost. Build a referral ask into every win milestone. Organic content: each piece of content depreciates slowly — unlike paid impressions that disappear when the budget stops. Conversion rate: the same ad budget producing twice the closes halves CAC without spending a dollar less.

✗Spend more on ads to get more leads. CAC stays the same, cash bleeds faster.
✓Improve the offer, double close rate, get twice the clients from the same spend. CAC drops in half.
07
LTGP : CACThe Flywheel

Better unit economics fund themselves.

When the ratio exceeds 3:1, the surplus unlocks speed. More cash-per-customer means you can out-bid competitors for the same lead. Your ad account is profitable at CPCs that send others to the sidelines.

High LTGP also means you can acquire customers at break-even — or even at a loss — because you know the back end pays it back. That’s the flywheel: a healthier ratio makes acquisition more aggressive, which drives volume, which improves your data, which tightens conversion, which lowers CAC further.

The ratio improves from both ends simultaneously once the engine is turning.

08
LTGP : CACThe Takeaway

Know the ratio. Widen the gap.

LTGP:CAC is the scoreboard underneath every business. If you don’t know your number today, calculate it this week. If it’s below 3:1, stop scaling ads and fix the unit. If it’s above 5:1, feed the machine as fast as you can afford.

The businesses that compound fastest aren’t the ones with the cleverest growth hacks. They’re the ones that quietly built a 5:1 ratio and never stopped improving it.
