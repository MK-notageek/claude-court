01
LEAD MAGNETSThe Definition

A lead magnet delivers a complete result for free.

Not a teaser. Not a sample chapter. A complete, standalone result that a motivated prospect would pay for.

Most lead magnets fail because they deliver hope — a vague promise that more value lives behind a paywall. A converting lead magnet delivers one complete win, so the prospect experiences your competence before paying a cent.

The test is simple: would a motivated buyer pay money for this? If yes, it qualifies as a lead magnet. If it’s just marginally useful, it won’t generate the urgency needed to give you an email address.

02
LEAD MAGNETSThe Five Types

Five formats. All of them deliver a complete result.

The format matters less than the result it delivers. Pick the format your ideal customer can consume fastest — because speed of value is one of the conversion levers.

Software tools convert highest because the prospect gets an immediate result the moment they use it. Information products scale best. Service samples create the shortest path to a paid conversion.

03
LEAD MAGNETSOne Step Removed

Solve the problem one step before your core offer does.

If your paid offer teaches people how to run Facebook ads, your lead magnet should solve the problem they hit just before running ads — not ads themselves. The magnet solves step one; the offer solves step two. The natural next move for anyone who completes the magnet is to buy.

This is why generic lead magnets leak. A guide called “10 Marketing Tips” has no natural next step. A guide called “How to Get Your First 50 Leads This Week” creates someone who now needs help managing those leads — which is your service.

04
LEAD MAGNETSThree Levers

Three levers that move opt-in rate.

All else equal, these three attributes determine whether a lead magnet earns a contact or gets scrolled past. Pull all three at once.

Pull all three simultaneously and you have a headline. “Get 10 booked calls this week using your existing contacts — no ads, takes 45 minutes.” That is specificity + certainty + speed in a single line.

05
LEAD MAGNETSThe Headline Formula

The headline formula that does all three jobs.

Once you have the concept, the headline writes itself. Plug in four tokens. If any slot is vague, the whole headline collapses. Be ruthlessly specific at each one.

The “without X” slot is the most underused. It removes the biggest objection before they’ve even opted in — which collapses the perceived effort barrier in the value equation.

06
LEAD MAGNETSTwo Quality Tests

Run it through two tests before you build it.

Both tests must pass. A lead magnet that fails either one will collect browsers, not buyers, and fill your CRM with names that never close.

Applying these two filters before you build saves you from producing content that drains your time and fills your list with people who will never buy.

07
LEAD MAGNETSThe Filter Function

The right magnet repels as much as it attracts.

A lead magnet that appeals to everyone attracts no one worth selling to. The specificity that raises your conversion rate also filters out prospects who could never buy — so your sales calls close at a higher rate and your list stays clean.

×Generic magnet: 10,000 opt-ins, 0.3% close rate — 30 customers from 10,000 contacts you have to manage.
✓Specific magnet: 1,200 opt-ins, 4% close rate — 48 customers from a list 88% smaller. More revenue, less noise.
08
LEAD MAGNETSThe Takeaway

One magnet. One audience. One complete result.

You don’t need ten lead magnets. You need one that passes both tests, uses all three levers, solves the problem one step before your core offer, and has a headline that could headline a paid product. That’s it. Build it once, drive traffic to it forever.
