01
UPSELLS, DOWNSELLS & CONTINUITYThe Opportunity

The biggest revenue leak is right at the sale.

Your customers are in peak buying mode the moment they hand over their card. Most businesses thank them and walk away. That’s the mistake.

A buyer who just purchased is already committed. They’ve made the hardest decision — to trust you with money. The next ask costs zero in ad spend and lands in the highest-intent moment in the entire customer journey.

02
UPSELLS, DOWNSELLS & CONTINUITYThe Upsell

Offer the logical next step up.

An upsell is an offer made immediately after the purchase decision — before the buyer has left the buying mindset. It answers one question: what would make this result faster, bigger, or easier?

The classic One-Time Offer (OTO) lives here. Not an artificial countdown timer — genuinely enhanced terms or additional elements available only right now, at checkout. The buyer is warm. The trust is established. Ask.

A well-placed upsell converts 20–30% of buyers. At 2× the core price, that’s a 40–60% increase in revenue from the same buyer — before they even receive delivery.

03
UPSELLS, DOWNSELLS & CONTINUITYThe Downsell

When they decline, go down, not away.

A downsell is the ask that comes when someone declines your upsell. Instead of ending the conversation, you offer a smaller, simpler, cheaper version of the same transformation — the thing they already said they wanted.

If your $297 upsell converts at 20%, a well-built $97 downsell converts another 12–15% of the decliners. That’s money that would have walked out the door. Never let a “no” be the end. Let it be the redirect.

04
UPSELLS, DOWNSELLS & CONTINUITYThe Recurring Engine

One-time sales fill the tank. Continuity drives the car.

A continuity program — membership, subscription, retainer — is the single highest-leverage element in any business. $97/month across 12 months is $1,164 vs. a $197 one-time sale. Most businesses choose the smaller number by accident.

And continuity compounds. Month 6 revenue includes every customer from months 1–5 who hasn’t cancelled. Your fixed costs are flat. Your margin grows with every cohort you retain.

05
UPSELLS, DOWNSELLS & CONTINUITYStack It

The four-layer revenue stack.

Each layer multiplies the value of the customer before they leave. Built in order of commitment — lowest barrier first, continuity last. Think of it as a staircase: you invite them up one step at a time, never skipping one.

The order matters. Continuity presented before the upsell feels like a subscription pitch before the result has been proven. Present it last — after they’ve won with your core offer and want to keep winning.

06
UPSELLS, DOWNSELLS & CONTINUITYThe Rules

Same person. Same moment. Better offer.

Most upsells fail because they break one of three rules. Stack all three and you get a clean yes without friction, buyer’s remorse, or refund requests.

✗“Also, would you like to buy our unrelated software tool?” — not congruent. Breaks the moment.
✓“Want us to build this for you instead of you doing it yourself?” — same topic, higher level.
✗“This is always available for $297.” — no urgency. No reason to decide now.
✓“Only at checkout — full price is $497, here it’s $297.” — genuine reason to act now.
07
UPSELLS, DOWNSELLS & CONTINUITYThe Math

Upsells are a math problem.

Assume 1,000 buyers at $97. Your upsell is $197 and converts at 20% — that’s $39,400 from people you already had. Add a $67 downsell at 10% of declines: +$5,360. Then a $97/month continuity at 30% take rate: $29,100 added to monthly revenue from month one, compounding forward.

The ad budget didn’t change. The audience didn’t change. The only change was asking at the right moments, in the right order, with the right framing. This is pure architecture, not sales pressure.

08
UPSELLS, DOWNSELLS & CONTINUITYDesign Continuity

Give them a reason to stay every month.

The best continuity delivers a specific, recurring win that compounds over time — a community that grows with them, a library that expands, a coaching structure that builds on itself. The moment you could pause the membership without missing it, that’s when customers cancel.

Price at the edge of “obvious yes” — where $97/month feels embarrassingly cheap relative to the outcome they’re sustaining. The price test: would they cancel if it doubled? If yes, it’s priced too high. If they’d laugh, it’s priced right.

UPSELLS, DOWNSELLS & CONTINUITYStack the Asks

Stack the asks. Capture the momentum.

The buying decision is already made. Every ask after the first one rides on that wave of commitment. You’re not selling them again — you’re inviting them deeper into a system they already said yes to.

Start with one: bolt a single upsell onto your checkout this week. Run 500 buyers through it. If 100+ take it, add the downsell next. Then continuity. Build the stack one layer at a time. Each layer funds the next.
