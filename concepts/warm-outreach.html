WARM OUTREACH & ACAWarm vs Cold

Cold traffic rents attention. Warm traffic owns it.

Cold outreach is expensive, slow, and adversarial. You’re buying time from strangers who didn’t ask to hear from you.

Warm outreach starts with a head start — they already know your name, your face, or your work. The trust floor is higher. The conversion rate is higher. The cost is zero.

Most people skip warm because it feels uncomfortable. That discomfort is the entire moat.

02
WARM OUTREACH & ACAThe Warm List

Your list is bigger than you think.

Most people imagine their warm audience is five friends. It isn’t. Anyone who knows your name — school, work, church, social media, clients past and present — is warm. Start counting.

Hormozi’s exercise: open your contacts. Write down every name. For most people that’s 200 to 500 people. Each one already trusts you more than a cold stranger ever will. That list is a business.

03
WARM OUTREACH & ACAThe ACA Framework

Three letters. Every warm message.

ACA — Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask. A structure so simple it takes thirty seconds to write but warm enough to convert at rates cold traffic never will.

The framework exists because most people skip the A and C and go straight to the ask. That’s why their messages feel salesy. Lead with them, and the ask feels earned.

04
WARM OUTREACH & ACAA — Acknowledge

Acknowledge: specific beats sincere.

Saying “I saw your post” is nothing. Saying “I saw your post about losing your first client and how you rebuilt from it — that took guts” stops them cold. Specificity signals you actually paid attention.

The acknowledge doesn’t have to be deep. It just has to be true and particular to this person right now. A recent post, a life event, a win you noticed — any of these work. Generic doesn’t.

05
WARM OUTREACH & ACAC — Compliment

Compliment: earn it or skip it.

Flattery is noise. It’s the stuff everyone says and no one believes. A real compliment names the specific thing they did that was genuinely good — and explains why.

The test: could you say the exact same compliment to a hundred other people? If yes, rewrite it. A compliment is specific to this person, this moment, this achievement. Anything else inflates the message and deflates the response rate.

06
WARM OUTREACH & ACAA — Ask

The ask: one variable, low friction.

After acknowledge and compliment, the ask feels natural — you’ve given before you took. Now the only rule is: one ask, clearly stated, framed as a question they can say yes or no to.

Don’t pitch. Don’t give a speech about your offer. Ask if they’d be open to learning more, or if they know someone who might benefit. Short is better. The goal is a response, not a sale — the sale happens on the call.

07
WARM OUTREACH & ACAVolume Negates Luck

Volume turns a hope into a machine.

Even at 20% conversion, you need to send messages to get calls. The math is simple: 100 warm messages × 20% reply = 20 conversations. Ten of those become calls. Three become clients.

Send 100 before you judge the channel. Most people send five, get two nos, and conclude warm outreach doesn’t work. It works. They stopped too soon.

WARM OUTREACH & ACAGo Do It Today

Open your contacts. Start messaging.

You don’t need a funnel. You don’t need ads. You don’t need a CRM. You need a phone with contacts and thirty seconds per message.

Set a daily goal: ten ACA messages before noon. In ten days you’ve sent 100. The clients are already in there — you just haven’t asked yet.
