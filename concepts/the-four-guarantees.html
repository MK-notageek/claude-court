THE FOUR GUARANTEESThe Real Enemy

You’re not competing with rivals.

The real enemy is inaction. The buyer already wants what you sell — they’re just afraid of being wrong.

Every prospect running through their head: “What if it doesn’t work for me?” That question blocks more sales than price, competition, or timing combined.

You’ve delivered this result a hundred times. They’re doing it for the first time. The side with more information should carry the risk. That side is you.

THE FOUR GUARANTEESThe Math

A guarantee is a math equation, not an emotional one.

Run the numbers before you panic about refunds. A strong guarantee typically lifts conversion 20% or more. Actual refund rates sit at 2–5% for most businesses.

The net is almost always positive. You’re leaving money on the table every day you don’t have one.

THE FOUR GUARANTEESThe Four Types

Four structures. One outcome: risk leaves the buyer.

Not all guarantees are the same. The right type depends on your offer, your confidence, your audience, and how much risk you can absorb. Each one signals something different.

The strongest signals the most confidence and lowers the bar to nothing. The most aligned signals “we win together.” The most exclusive says “it works — that’s why there is no refund.”

04
THE FOUR GUARANTEESType 01 — Unconditional

The strongest move: no questions asked.

An unconditional guarantee removes every last obstacle. There is no condition, no checklist, no proof required. The buyer either loves it or gets their money back — full stop.

This sounds terrifying. It rarely is. The business that has a strong product and delivers what it promises will see refund rates under 5% even with an ironclad unconditional. Worried customers who would have never bought now become customers who rarely refund.

Best for: digital products, info products, low-touch services where delivery is immediate and refund fraud is manageable.

05
THE FOUR GUARANTEESType 02 — Conditional

Bake your best customer’s success path into the guarantee.

A conditional guarantee adds requirements — but that’s a feature, not a bug. The conditions are the exact steps your best customers take to get the result. If someone follows them and still fails, you genuinely haven’t delivered and the refund is warranted.

Engineer it backward: look at your top 20% of customers. What did they do? What milestones did they hit? Write those as the conditions. Fewest conditions wins — three or fewer is ideal.

Best for: coaching, courses, high-touch services — where outcome depends partly on buyer action.

06
THE FOUR GUARANTEESType 03 — Anti-Guarantee

The boldest play: no refund, on purpose.

An anti-guarantee flips the logic entirely. Instead of lowering the risk of buying, it raises the signal of confidence so high that the risk disappears. “It works. That’s why we don’t offer refunds.”

This sounds counterintuitive — and it is. But in the right context, with the right audience and a proven product, it works precisely because most products hide behind vague guarantees. The anti-guarantee says: there is no safety net because none is needed.

Best for: high-ticket offers, exclusive programs, or businesses with overwhelming social proof and a clearly earned reputation.

07
THE FOUR GUARANTEESType 04 — Implied

The aligned play: we only win if you win.

An implied guarantee is embedded in the deal structure itself. You don’t call it a guarantee. There’s no refund clause. But the economics make it clear: if the buyer doesn’t succeed, you don’t get paid.

Revenue share, performance bonuses, equity stakes — all are implied guarantees. They signal total alignment. The prospect’s internal monologue shifts from “will this work?” to “if it doesn’t work, they lose too.”

Best for: agencies, growth services, consulting — anywhere your output directly drives a measurable client result.

08
THE FOUR GUARANTEESStacking

One guarantee is good. Stacked guarantees are a machine.

You don’t have to pick one. Layer a short-term unconditional with a longer-term conditional. The first covers the hesitant buyer. The second covers the motivated one who needs proof of commitment on your side.

The combined message: “Try it risk-free for 30 days. And if you do the work and don’t hit the result in 90 days, we’re not done.”

THE FOUR GUARANTEESWrite It Right

The words make it real or invisible.

Vague guarantees get banner-blindness. Every seller says “results guaranteed.” Nobody believes it because it means nothing. Your guarantee needs to name the exact outcome, the exact timeframe, and the exact consequence if it doesn’t happen.

You don’t even need the word “guarantee.” Describing precisely what you will do if the result isn’t delivered is stronger than any boilerplate phrase.

×“We guarantee your satisfaction.”
×“Results guaranteed or your money back.”
✓“If you don’t add at least $10,000 to your monthly revenue in 90 days following the program, I’ll write you a check for the difference.”
