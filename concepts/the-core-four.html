THE CORE FOURThe Map

Leads come from exactly four places.

Two questions decide everything. Do they know you yet — warm or cold? And are you reaching one person or many at once?

Cross those two axes and you get a grid with four boxes. There is no fifth box. Every billion-dollar ad strategy and every scrappy cold DM lives somewhere on this map.

Don’t try to run all four at once. Pick the one nearest you — warm outreach — and earn the right to the harder boxes.

02
THE CORE FOUR1 · Warm Outreach

Warm outreach: start where you’re trusted.

The fastest leads aren’t strangers. They’re the people who already have your number. Your phone has more buried opportunity than any ad account you’ll ever build.

But you can’t open with the pitch. Lead with them. Acknowledge, compliment, then ask — the ACA framework. Give before you take, every single time.

Work the whole list. Twenty-five names a day, every day. Most won’t buy — but they’ll point you to someone who will, and that referral runs warm too.

03
THE CORE FOUR2 · Post Content

Post content: give value to the many.

Warm outreach taps people one at a time. Content taps your whole audience at once — for free, forever. You give value publicly, and the right strangers raise their hands.

Every post does the same four jobs, on a loop. Hook them in, retain their attention, reward them with something useful, then repeat — tomorrow, and the day after.

Content is slow to start and impossible to stop. One viewer becomes a follower, a follower becomes a buyer — and the back catalogue keeps working while you sleep.

04
THE CORE FOUR3 · Cold Outreach

Cold outreach: strangers, by the thousand.

Now you contact people who have no idea who you are — one at a time, but at brutal scale. The math is simple and unforgiving: big list, big volume, a lead magnet good enough to earn a reply.

Each step shrinks. Thousands of names become hundreds of messages that land, dozens of responses, a handful of real leads. So you fix the leak by pouring more in the top.

Don’t agonize over the perfect line to a single prospect. Personalize the first sentence, automate the rest, and send until the numbers move. Volume is the strategy.

05
THE CORE FOUR4 · Paid Ads

Paid ads: rent the strangers’ attention.

The last box is the most powerful and the most expensive. You pay a platform to put your message in front of strangers — one-to-many, on demand. Money becomes attention becomes leads.

Every ad that works is built the same way. Call out the right person, deliver value so they keep watching, then give one clear CTA. Three bands, in that order.

Ads scale faster than anything else and punish sloppiness hardest. Earn your reps in the free three boxes first, then pour fuel on the fire that’s already burning.

06
THE CORE FOURThe Rule Of 100

The Rule of 100: activity guarantees outcomes.

Strategy fails for one reason — not enough at-bats. So fix the variable you control. Do 100 primary actions a day — 100 reach-outs, 100 minutes of content, 100 ad dollars — for 100 days straight.

You can’t control replies, the algorithm, or click-through rates. You can control how many shots you take. Take a hundred a day and luck stops mattering.

07
THE CORE FOURScale It

Scale any channel: more, better, new.

Once a box works, you scale it in exactly three moves — and in this order. Do more of what works. Make each action better. Only then add a new channel.

Most people jump straight to a shiny new channel and stay broke across four mediocre ones. Squeeze the box you have until it screams — then open the next.

08
THE CORE FOURThe Takeaway

One box. A hundred a day. That’s it.

You don’t have a lead problem. You have a volume problem disguised as a strategy problem. The map is settled — four boxes, no more. The only open question is whether you do the reps.
