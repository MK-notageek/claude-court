WHAT · WHO · WHENThe Three Variables

Three levers. Only the intersection converts.

Most businesses get WHAT right — they build a solid offer. They miss WHO and WHEN entirely. The offer lands in silence because it’s aimed at the wrong person at the wrong moment.

Think of it as a three-way Venn diagram. A great offer (WHAT) + a precise avatar (WHO) + perfect timing (WHEN) = sales that feel effortless. Pull any one variable out of alignment and conversion collapses.

02
WHAT · WHO · WHENWHAT — Match Offer to Awareness

The right offer depends on where they are.

A cold prospect who doesn’t know they have a problem can’t buy a high-ticket solution. They need a different entry point — something that first names the problem, then introduces the solution.

Awareness drives offer type. A hot prospect who’s already shopping needs proof and comparison. A cold prospect needs to first feel the pain.

The mistake is running your best high-ticket offer to an unaware audience. Not because they wouldn’t want it — they don’t know they need it yet.

03
WHAT · WHO · WHENWHO — The Avatar in Detail

“Business owners” is not an avatar.

The tighter your definition of WHO, the less work your copy has to do. A precise avatar means the person reads your message and thinks: “This is about me.” That feeling is priceless.

Five dimensions to lock in: industry/niche, revenue range, team size, specific problem they have right now, and what they’ve already tried that hasn’t worked.

You can only write that specifically if you’ve worked with dozens of them. If you haven’t, go serve ten people and come back with data.

04
WHAT · WHO · WHENWHEN — The Problem Timeline

Timing the offer to acute pain.

The same person with the same problem at different points on their journey is a completely different buyer. Catch them at the wrong moment and even a perfect offer gets a polite “maybe later.”

The sweet spot is acute pain. They’ve recognised the problem, tried something, and failed. The cost of inaction is now higher than the cost of action. That’s when your offer has the lowest resistance.

Find these people by looking for recent failures — job postings that went quiet, software that got cancelled, courses that got abandoned.

05
WHAT · WHO · WHENAwareness in Practice

Different stages need different language.

The words that convert a “Most Aware” buyer will confuse an “Unaware” prospect. The language has to meet them where they are. Use their words, not yours.

The diagnostic signal: if people are asking “what is this?” — you’re talking to people who aren’t ready. If people are asking “how soon can we start?” — you’ve matched the stage.

06
WHAT · WHO · WHENThe Starving Crowd

A starving crowd beats a brilliant offer.

If you were opening a hot dog cart and could pick either the best hot dogs in the world or the best location in the world — pick the location every time. The starving crowd always wins.

Find people already buying. They’ve already decided to solve the problem. The only question left is which vendor. Now your offer is a comparison, not a conversion — and that’s a much easier sale.

07
WHAT · WHO · WHENAll Three in Alignment

When all three align, selling feels like serving.

Right offer to the wrong person at the wrong time = silence. Right person, wrong time = “call me in six months.” Right person, right time, wrong offer = “interesting, but not for me.”

All three together: the prospect feels like you read their mind. They say “how did you know I needed this?” That’s not magic — it’s targeting.

The audit question: write down your current WHAT, WHO, and WHEN. If any is vague, your conversion is leaking from that variable.

08
WHAT · WHO · WHENThe Targeting Audit

Run the three-variable audit.

Every campaign that falls flat has a misaligned variable. The fix isn’t more volume — it’s diagnosis. Write down your current WHAT, WHO, and WHEN and ask one question for each.

WHAT: Would someone at this exact awareness level recognise this offer as being for them? WHO: Could you walk into a room and point at the person? WHEN: Are they in pain right now?
