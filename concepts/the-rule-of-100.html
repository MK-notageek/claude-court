The Rule of 100
01
THE RULE OF 100The Real Problem

You don’t have a strategy problem. You have a volume problem.

Every stuck founder believes the offer is broken, the niche is wrong, the funnel needs another tool. Almost none of them are right.

The funnel is fine. The offer is good enough. The copy works. Nobody is pulling the lever enough times to find out. A hundred attempts at almost anything will teach you more than a thousand hours of planning ever could.

Fix the volume before you touch the strategy. Most “bad offers” are just offers that haven’t been heard by enough people, enough times.

02
THE RULE OF 100Primary Action

One action per channel. The one most connected to leads.

Every channel has a single move that actually generates contact with potential buyers. Not prep, not research, not editing. The one act that, at high volume, eventually puts a prospect in your funnel.

Everything else — analytics reviews, A/B tests, script revisions — is maintenance. Do the hundred first; maintenance fills the gaps around it.

03
THE RULE OF 100The Daily Number

Not “some.” Not “a lot.” One hundred.

The number is not arbitrary — it is a commitment device. Ten feels like enough. A hundred forces you to build the systems, the scripts, the calendar blocks that make it repeatable. You can’t do 100 on willpower alone.

A hundred is a concrete finish line you can see from the start of the day. Anything vague lets you rationalize stopping at 47.

04
THE RULE OF 100The 100-Day Test

100 days is the minimum honest test.

Most people quit in the first thirty days because results lag behind effort. The math of compounding means you will feel nothing for weeks, then everything at once. Quitting before day 60 is quitting before the experiment begins.

At day 100 you will have more data than most businesses collect in a year. Then you evaluate whether to continue, optimize, or switch channels.

05
THE RULE OF 100Volume Creates Data

You can’t optimize what you haven’t done a hundred times.

Every tweak to your message, every change to your hook, every new subject line — none of it is meaningful until you have a large enough sample to read a pattern. Low volume produces noise. High volume produces signal.

The fastest path to a high-converting offer is not a smarter launch plan. It’s 100 reps, fast — then one targeted optimization, repeated.

06
THE RULE OF 100The Flywheel

Volume feeds the flywheel that compounds your skill.

Every rep produces a result — a reply, a no, a click, a sign-up. Every result teaches you something. Every insight lets you tighten the message. A tighter message gets a better result next time. That loop is where skill actually lives.

Expertise is not talent. It’s reps × reflection × adjustment — run in a loop, at speed. Volume is not opposed to quality. Volume is what creates it.

07
THE RULE OF 100Replace Motivation

Motivation dips. Systems don’t.

You cannot do 100 primary actions per day on enthusiasm alone. In week one it feels possible. In week three it feels pointless. The rule only works if it is backed by a system that runs whether or not you feel like it.

×Waiting until you feel inspired to do outreach — so some days you do 3, some days 30, and the average is 8.
✓Morning block, scripted template, 100 messages sent before anything else opens. Boring, systematic, and it compounds.

Build the script. Book the block. Track the number. The only creative decision is what the 100 actions are — not whether you do them.

08
THE RULE OF 100The Takeaway

Pick one channel. Do 100. For 100 days.

You don’t need a new strategy. You need to stop treating the current one as optional. Commit one channel, commit 100 primary actions per day, and do not evaluate the results until you have 100 days of data. That is 10,000 reps. No serious business judgment can be made with less.
