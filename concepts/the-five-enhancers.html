THE FIVE ENHANCERSThe Map

A good offer plus five levers.

The value equation builds an offer worth buying. The enhancers make it impossible to put down.

Each enhancer attacks a different psychological barrier. One compresses supply, one compresses time, one stacks perceived value, one removes risk, and one makes the offer stick in memory. Together they create urgency, desire, confidence, and recall — the four forces behind a purchase decision.

Use them in sequence. Build the offer first. Then stack the enhancers. Never the reverse.

02
THE FIVE ENHANCERS01 · Scarcity

Scarcity: limited supply.

When supply is constrained, value rises. Not because the product changed — because availability changed. Humans weight losses more heavily than gains, so the threat of missing out moves people faster than the promise of getting in.

Real scarcity is always better than manufactured scarcity. Limit cohort size because you can only serve 20 well. Limit bonuses because they expire naturally. Limit the price because you’re raising it.

The rule: if you announce scarcity, enforce it. A deadline that resets is a discount in disguise — and it trains buyers to wait forever.

03
THE FIVE ENHANCERS02 · Urgency

Urgency: limited time.

Scarcity compresses supply. Urgency compresses time. A deadline forces a decision — every day that passes without one is a day the prospect talked themselves out of buying.

Four types of legitimate urgency: cohort-based (next intake closes Monday), price-based (rates increase as demand does), bonus-based (fast-action gifts disappear at midnight), and seasonal (end of financial year, launch window). Any one of these is enough.

The failure mode: soft deadlines. “Act soon” means nothing. A date, a time, a consequence — that’s urgency. Vague urgency is just filler copy.

04
THE FIVE ENHANCERS03 · Bonuses

Bonuses: stack the value.

People rarely buy the core offer alone — they buy the stack. Bonuses raise perceived value far above their cost, because the buyer sees the “what I get” list, not your cost of delivery.

Three rules for high-converting bonuses. One: each bonus should address a specific objection or fear the buyer holds. Two: name and price every bonus individually so the total becomes undeniable. Three: make at least one bonus more exciting than the core offer — it makes the sale easy.

A poorly chosen bonus dilutes the offer. “We’ll throw in a mug” signals low confidence. Each bonus must solve a real problem that exists on the path to the outcome.

05
THE FIVE ENHANCERS04 · Guarantees

Guarantees: reverse the risk.

The first three enhancers create desire. Guarantees destroy the last reason to hesitate. Your prospect has been burned before. They believe you, they want the outcome, but they won’t bet against their track record of disappointment.

A guarantee says: I’ll carry that risk, not you. You’ve done this a hundred times. This is their first. The more you know the outcome, the more you can afford the guarantee.

Be specific. Vague promises get tuned out. The more precisely you name the outcome you guarantee — with a number, a timeline, a condition — the more believable and powerful it becomes.

06
THE FIVE ENHANCERS05 · Naming

Naming: make it stick.

A generic name loses sales before anyone hears the pitch. A magnetic name communicates the dream outcome before the prospect even asks “what is it?”. The MAGIC formula builds that name.

The formula has five parts. Magnet: who it’s for. Avatar: the specific dream outcome. Goal: the timeframe. Indicator: the mechanism. Container: what it is (system, blueprint, sprint, formula).

You don’t need all five in every name. Hit three or four and you have something worth printing on a slide. It should be something you can say in one breath and they can repeat back correctly.

07
THE FIVE ENHANCERSStack Them

Stack all five. Leave nothing on the table.

The enhancers multiply each other. Scarcity and urgency together create a two-sided vice: not enough spots, and the clock is running. Bonuses make the stack feel lopsided in the buyer’s favour. A guarantee neutralises the remaining fear. A magnetic name makes it easy to say yes out loud.

The order matters. Build the core offer first. A bad offer with five enhancers is just a flashier bad offer. The enhancers amplify — they don’t save.

GOOD OFFER × FIVE ENHANCERS = IRRESISTIBLE.
bad offer × five enhancers = faster rejection

THE FIVE ENHANCERSThe Takeaway

You’re one layer away from unstoppable.

Most businesses stop at the core offer and wonder why they’re fighting for every sale. The enhancers aren’t manipulation — they’re respect. You’re removing every rational and emotional reason to delay a decision that would genuinely help someone.

Start with real scarcity. Add a real deadline. Stack three bonuses that target real objections. Guarantee the outcome you’re confident in. Name it so people say it in their sleep. Now sell.
