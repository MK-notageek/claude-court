01
GIVE UNTIL THEY ASKThe Asking Problem

Everyone asks. Almost nobody gives.

The default sales move is to ask — for attention, for a meeting, for money — before the prospect has any reason to say yes. It’s backwards.

When you ask without giving first, you’re asking strangers to trust you on credit. Every “buy now” message sent to a cold audience is a withdrawal from an account that has zero balance.

Giving first deposits trust before the ask. The ask becomes easy — even obvious — because the prospect already knows what you’re worth.

02
GIVE UNTIL THEY ASKThe Give Ratio

How much should you give?

Hormozi’s heuristic: give until it feels uncomfortable, then give more. Most people hit a point where they think “if I give this away, why would they pay me?” That feeling is the signal to keep going.

Giving away your best ideas does not reduce your value. It demonstrates it. The person who consumes your free content and still can’t do it themselves — that’s your buyer. Knowledge creates desire; it does not replace the need for help.

03
GIVE UNTIL THEY ASKWhat to Give

Four things worth giving.

Not all giving is equal. Give the things your ideal customer actually needs before they pay you. The best gift is information that removes a belief barrier or solves a real problem they’re struggling with right now.

The goal is not to make them feel warm and fuzzy. It’s to make them feel capable — and then realize they still need your help to execute.

04
GIVE UNTIL THEY ASKWhere to Give

Give through every channel.

Giving is a strategy, not a format. The vehicle matters less than the consistency. Short-form video, long essays, DMs, community posts — any medium works if the content is genuinely useful to the right person.

Pick the platform where your ideal customer already spends time. Then show up daily. The compound effect of consistent giving means that by month six, prospects have received more value from you than from most paid products.

05
GIVE UNTIL THEY ASKVolume Is the Game

Volume isn’t vanity. It’s math.

One great post reaches one moment in one feed. One hundred consistent posts reaches hundreds of micro-moments over months. Trust is not built in a single transaction — it’s built through repeated, quality exposure.

More giving means more at-bats. The prospect who sees 30 posts before DMing you is already convinced. The conversation skips past “who are you?” straight to “when can we start?”

06
GIVE UNTIL THEY ASKThe Transition

When and how to ask.

You earn the right to ask by giving first. But you still have to ask — clearly, specifically, with a single call to action. The ask is not a pitch. It’s an invitation.

The best ask is an extension of the giving: “I’ve shared how this framework works. If you want me to apply it to your specific situation, reply here.” No pressure, no urgency tricks — just a natural next step for someone who already trusts you.

You don’t wait until you’re done giving to start asking. Give daily. Ask once a week. The ratio holds throughout.

07
GIVE UNTIL THEY ASKFull Comparison

The business you build is different.

Running a give-first model changes more than your conversion rate. It changes the kind of customers you attract, the conversations you have, and the price you can charge.

Customers who found you through months of free value arrive pre-sold, pre-qualified, and grateful. They don’t negotiate price. They don’t need convincing. They came to you.

08
GIVE UNTIL THEY ASKStart Giving

Start today. Give first.

Pick the format you can sustain: a daily post, a weekly deep-dive, a public Q&A. Then show up, every day, with something genuinely useful — not a teaser, not a hook that withholds. The full thing.

The people who consume the most free content and still can’t do it themselves are your ideal buyers. Giving freely pre-qualifies them and pre-sells them simultaneously.
