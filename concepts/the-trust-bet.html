01
THE TRUST BETThe Definition

Trust is not a feeling. It’s a bet.

Someone says “just trust me” — and you freeze. It’s an odd command. What are they actually asking for?

Here is the answer, behaviorally. To trust someone is to make yourself punishable by them.

You hand over a secret, your word, or a need. That thing is now leverage — something they could use to hurt you. Then you place a bet: they won’t.

That’s the whole definition. Look at any relationship and you can name the exact moment trust occurred: when someone made themselves punishable and wasn’t punished.

02
THE TRUST BETThe Two Dials

Every act of trust has two dials.

It feels like one fuzzy thing. It’s actually two clean questions — and the answers change everything.

Who is at risk — you, or them? And who does the punishing if the bet goes bad — the other person, or reality itself?

Those two dials, with two positions each, are the entire map. Turn the page.

03
THE TRUST BETThe Four Types

The four types of trust.

Set the two dials and every trust you’ve ever extended falls into one of these boxes.

Most arguments about trust are really two people standing in different boxes — one talking secrets, the other talking reliability.

04
THE TRUST BETThe Two Tests

So should you trust them?

Before you make yourself punishable, run two tests. Both have to pass.

One — track record. Given the chance to burn you, have they protected you instead? A knife at your back they chose not to use?

Two — incentive. Does burning you cost them more than keeping you? If betrayal is a bad deal for them, you’re far safer.

Track record without aligned incentive is luck. Incentive without track record is a guess.
05
THE TRUST BETThe Ceiling

Then why risk it at all?

Because the ceiling of any relationship is set by one thing: how much context the two of you share.

A stranger can barely help you — they know nothing about you. Every single thing you reveal hands them more to work with, and lifts the ceiling on what you can become to each other.

That’s the blade. The more someone can help you, the more they can hurt you. You don’t get the upside without handing over the downside.

06
THE TRUST BETZero Punishment

Trust runs on zero punishment.

You give a little. They don’t misuse it. You give a little more. Still safe. Drop by drop, over years, the trust compounds into something huge.

Then one day the short-term temptation — winning the argument, using what they told you — beats the long game. And they take it.

The betrayal doesn’t subtract from the trust. It resets it to zero — and undoes every cycle that built it.

It’s a tree cut from the vine. It still stands for a while — but the nutrients, the truth flowing between you, are already severed. Everyone knows when it’s hollow inside.

07
THE TRUST BETThe Payoff

So be the one who never punishes.

Trustworthiness isn’t soft. It’s leverage. Refuse to punish what people hand you — and they hand you more.

The litmus — both directions
To trust someone: check the track record, then the incentive. Both must pass.
To be trusted: zero punishment — when they disclose, and forever after. Keep your word.
Make yourself punishable. Then never make them regret it.
