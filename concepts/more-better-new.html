01
MORE, BETTER, NEWThe Three Levers

Three levers. One sequence.

When leads slow down, most founders reach for something new. A new platform, a new tactic, a new agency. That impulse is almost always wrong.

There are exactly three ways to get more customers from any channel. More volume. Better conversion. New channels. The mistake is the order — jumping to New before exhausting the first two.

Pull them in sequence and a single channel can take you to seven figures before you ever touch a second one.

02
MORE, BETTER, NEWLever 1 · More

MORE: volume is the first diagnosis.

Before anything else, ask: am I doing enough? Most people aren’t. They send ten cold emails, get no replies, and conclude cold email doesn’t work. The channel didn’t fail — the volume did.

Hormozi’s Rule of 100 puts a number on it: 100 primary actions per day, for 100 days. Not ten. Not fifty. A hundred. Volume is the only variable guaranteed to produce a data set you can act on.

Do more of what works before you touch anything else. The answer is almost always sitting inside the existing channel, starved of reps.

03
MORE, BETTER, NEWLever 2 · Better

BETTER: now make each rep count more.

Once you’re doing the volume, look at the rate. What percentage of contacts become replies? Replies become calls? Calls become clients? Every conversion point is a lever you can tighten.

A 3% close rate on 100 outreaches gives you 3 leads. Improve the rate to 6% — same volume, twice the leads. Better is a multiplier on More.

This is where message quality, offer clarity, and follow-up sequences live. You can’t improve what you haven’t measured — so the reps from More give you the data that Better needs.

04
MORE, BETTER, NEWLever 3 · New

NEW: add a channel last, not first.

Only after you’ve pushed volume high and tightened conversion should you look at a new channel. By then you have something most founders don’t: a playbook that actually works.

A new channel multiplies a working system — but it amplifies a broken one just as fast. The discipline is earning the right to expand before you expand.

05
MORE, BETTER, NEWThe Shiny Channel Trap

The trap is shiny and familiar.

A channel produces one good lead, then goes quiet. The instinct is to call the channel dead and go hunting for the next one. But the channel isn’t dead — the volume wasn’t there, or the message wasn’t sharp enough.

Jumping to a new channel feels like progress because it removes the discomfort of doing the boring, grinding volume work on the existing one. That comfort is the trap.

06
MORE, BETTER, NEWThe Compounding Effect

Each lever multiplies the last.

More × Better × New isn’t addition — it’s multiplication. You don’t add 30 + 30 + 30. You multiply volume by rate by channels.

100 actions/day at 3% close on one channel = 3 leads. Push to 100 actions at 6% close = 6. Add a second channel at the same rate = 12 leads a day from the same inputs, multiplied three ways.

The sequence enforces this arithmetic. Jumping to New early just distributes a low rate across more channels.

07
MORE, BETTER, NEWWhich Lever to Pull

Ask two questions. Pull the right lever.

The diagnostic is simple. Start with volume. If you’re not at 100 primary actions per day, you haven’t earned the right to blame the channel — do more.

If you’re already at volume, look at the rate. Where does the funnel leak? Low reply rate = message problem. Low close rate = offer problem. Low retention = delivery problem. Fix the leak before pouring more in.

Only when volume is maxed and rate is tight does adding a new channel make sense. By that point you’re not guessing — you have a repeatable system to transplant.

08
MORE, BETTER, NEWThe Takeaway

One channel, done right, is enough.

You don’t have a channel problem. You have a discipline problem dressed up as a strategy problem. The sequence is settled — More, then Better, then New. The only open question is whether you grind each step before reaching for the next.

Pick your primary channel. Commit to 100 actions a day for 100 days before evaluating. That single constraint will out-earn every new tactic your competitors are chasing.
